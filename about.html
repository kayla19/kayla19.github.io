Hello! I'm Nakayla, or Kayla, Smith. I'm currently a Service Desk Engineer at Wayfair. I attended Northeastern University as a Computer Engineering major, but left school after earning 95/131 credits. I was chosen for the Ujima Global Leaders Scholarship, a merit scholarship through the John D. O'Bryant African American Institute. I previously worked on campus in the Synergetic Media Learning Lab as an undergraduate researcher, helping with the Computer Vision project, and the Connections Computer Lab as a lab assistant. This year, I am co-oping at MIT Lincoln Laboratory with Group 92s: Advanced Sensors and Techniques.

On campus, I am involved with the student chapters of IEEE, National Society of Black Engineers, and Wireless Club. I served as the Community Outreach Coordinator for IEEE in Fall 2015, and as the Secretary of IEEE in Spring 2016. I volunteer for the Center for STEM involvement on campus. In February 2017, I will be teaching a beginning electrical engineering course to girls in Brockton with the non-profit KodeConnect.

I attended Agua Fria High School in Avondale, Arizona, graduating in 2014 at the top 5% of my class with honors from National Honor Society. While there, I was enrolled in AP and honors classes and admitted into the gifted and talented program. I won an AP Scholar award for my high scores on various AP tests, and was a National Achievement Scholarship semifinalist. I was heavily involved in the music program, playing baritone saxophone in Jazz Band, bass clarinet in Symphonic Band, and soprano clarinet in marching band. I also briefly played the 5-head tenor drums in Winter Drumline. My senior year, I served the band as the secretary of band council and the clarinet section leader. That spring, I auditioned for the statewide honors band on the bass clarinet and made second chair. I was awarded the director's Philip Sousa award for musical excellence.

You can reach me at hello@nakay.la.
